5. Study Overview 
This research study is being conducted to document and describe languages of the world. We are inviting you to participate in the research study because you are a speaker or signer of  ________________________________________________________________________________________.  If you agree to be in this study, you will be asked to do one or more of the following:  
1. answer questions about your language and how you use it, including how to express words or signs,  sentences or concepts in your language  
2. tell a story or other form of performance (poetry, riddle, song) in your language, have a conversation  in your language with other speaker(s) or signer(s), or allow the researchers to record you using your  language in a natural setting in your community  
3. read a list of words or sentences or describe pictures or videos in your language.  Breaks are given at any point you wish. 
This form explains the research so that you may make an informed decision about participating.  
• Research is voluntary - whether or not you participate is your decision. You can discuss your decision  with others. 
• You can say yes, but change your mind later. 
• If you say no, we will not hold your decision against you. 
• Please ask the study team questions about anything that is not clear, and feel free to ask questions  and mention concerns before, during, and after the research. 
• You will be given a copy of this consent form and the Participant’s Bill of Rights.

We cannot promise any benefit to you or to others from you participating in this research. However, possible  benefits include a greater knowledgeof the use and structure of your language.  
The alternative to being in this study is not to participate.

7. How many people will take part? 
The number of people who will take part in the research is not predetermined.  
8. What are the risks and possible discomforts? 
The most common risks or discomforts of this study are boredom and fatigue. Participants may request a  break at any point. There are no known serious risks.  
9. How will information about me be protected? 
It is customary practice in language documentation research to publicly acknowledgethose who participation  any output resulting from the research. You will be given the opportunity at the end of this consent form to  allow the researcher to acknowledge your participation.  
In case you decide not to have your name publicly acknowledged, while we cannot guarantee complete  confidentiality, we will limit access to information about you. Only people who have a need to review your  information or documents will have access. These people include members of the research team and other  staff or representatives of UCSD whose work is related to the research or to protecting your rights.  
Study information will be labeled with a code instead of your name or other information that can easily  identify you. The record linking your identifying information and the code will be kept separate from the rest  of the study information. 
The results of this study may be published once the study is completed. However, we will keep your name and  other identifying information confidential.  
10. Will I need to pay to participate in the research? 
There is no cost to you to participate in the research.

Experimental Participant's Bill of Rights 
Every individual asked to participate in a research study has the right to be:  
1. Informed about the nature and purpose of the study.  
2. Provided an explanation ofthe procedures to be followed in the research study.  
3. Given a description of any side effects, discomforts, or risks that you can reasonably expect to occur  during the study.  
4. Informed about any benefits that would reasonably be expected from the participation in the study, if  applicable.  
5. Provided an opportunity to ask any questions concerning the research study both before agreeing to  participate and at any time during the course of the study.  
6. Informed that individuals can refuse to participate in the research study. Participation is voluntary.  Research participantsmay refuse to answer any question or discontinue their involvement at any time  without penalty or loss of benefits to which they might otherwise be entitled. Theirdecision will not  affect their right to receive the care they would receive if they were not in the experiment.  
7. Provided a copy of this form.  
8. Given the opportunity to freely decide whether or not to consent to the research study without any  force, coercion, or undue influence.
